Essence of Time

There is no time to waste. By 2025 the average amount of waste produced per day is to double from 3 million tons to 6 million tons. Added with the factor of improper waste management, the Earth will not be able to contain the effects it is causing.

Climate Change

40% of the world’s trash is burned, releasing carbon dioxide into the atmosphere and contributing to global warming. When trash is dumped into landfills, it releases methane gas creating a dangerous ground-level ozone.

Wildlife

With the amount of trash and plastic in the oceans biodiversity is hindered. Aquatic animals mistake trash for food ingesting those fragments and cannot break it down due to their low acidic levels. This affects their health and may even lead to death. Similarly, land animals mistake trash for food and can be poisoned in the process.

Health

With more and more trash being produced, humans increase their risk for certain diseases and bacterial infections. Long-term exposure can disrupt the endocrine system and cause respiratory problems.
